The diverse landscape of Belize offers visitors much to see and do.

The towns and cities in mainland belize are great for ecotourism jungle adventures, while the islands/cayes and coastal villages are perfect for sun, sand and sea activities. Fortunately the country is very small and you'll be able to visit both jungle and beach destinations to experience the most of Belize in a short amount of time.


1. Belize City

With the only international airport in Belize, Belize City acts as the main gateway into the country. It is also known as the old capital and has a lot of historical sites such as the Belize Zoo, The Baron Bliss Lighthouse, the House of Culture, the Museum of Belize, the Supreme Court, and Holy Redeemer Cathedral. Belize City is the larget city in Belize and is the country's center of commerce. The city is surrounded by the Caribbean Sea so you are in for some very scenic drives in the city. The Belize River which is the biggest river in the country also runs through the city. Belize City is home to most hotel chains in Belize, and even the first cruise terminal.


2. Ambergris Caye

Ambergris Caye is Belize's largest island and the most popular tourist destination in the country. San Pedro is the name of the only town on the island and it has a lively downtown area with lots of nice restaurants and beach bars.The most popular activity to do on Ambergris Caye is world-class diving and snorkeling since the barrier reef is less than 1 mile away, and the main mode of travel on the island is to use golf carts or bicycles.


3. The Maya Ruins

At the height of the Mayan Empire, large cities were built in what is now Belize that housed hundred of thousands of people. The Maya used the Belize River as a "super highway" to transport good throughout the country and connect with the big city-states in Guatemala.

In addition to the well-excavated ruins such as Xunantunich and Caracol pictured above, there are many other large cities that are still awaiting exploration in the jungles and rainforests.

Today, many ethnic Maya continue to live in Belize, particularly in the southern parts of the country.


4. The Great Blue Hole

The Great Blue Hole is a large underwater sinkhole off the coast of Belize. It lies near the center of Lighthouse Reef, a small atoll 62 miles from the mainland of Belize City. The hole is circular in shape, over 984 feet across and 410 feet deep.

The Great Blue Hole is a vast expanse of interconnected underwater caverns adorned with remarkable formations such as stalactites, stalagmites, dropstone sheets, and columns. The marine life in the sinkhole consists of nurse sharks, giant groupers, and several types of reef sharks such as the Caribbean reef shark and the Blacktip shark.

Dive excursions to the Great Blue Hole are full-day trips that usually consist of one dive in the Blue Hole and two additional dives in nearby reefs.
