Commonly Used Words and Phrases in Belize
❮ ❯

Although English is the official language of the country, a dialect called Creole (or Kriol) is spoken by most Belizeans. It's the first thing you will hear when you arrive in Belize - the accent, the intonation, and the sentences that chop away at articles and verbs. Most English speaking Caribbean countries such as Jamaica, Barbados, Trinidad and Tobago, Guyana, and more have a dialect similar to the Creole spoken in Belize. Creole finds its roots in the days when the enslaved workers were exposed to English and mixed it with their own West African dialects, hence the choppy grammar and the borrowed English words.

Over time, efforts were made to ensure that Kriol was properly studied, written, and recorded as a language, thanks to the National Kriol Council.


Basic Phrases in Belizean Kriol

  • Gud maanin! - Good morning!
  • Weh di guh aan? - What's up!
  • Da weh yuh naym? - What's your name?
  • Weh paat...? - Where is...? e.g. Weh paat dih shoe deh?
  • Fu chroo? - Really?
  • Weh yuh di seh? - What are you saying?

Commonly used words in Belizean Kriol

  • Pikni - Children
  • Madaz - My Gosh!
  • Unu - The Belizean equivalent of y'all
  • Bredda - The Belizean equivalent of bro and/or bruh
  • Bally - Used when referring to a guy e.g. Dih bally weh I mih gaan da skool wid
  • Bore - to make a hole e.g. Mayn yuh bore dih page
  • Buck - run into e.g. I wa buck unu layta; I buck into she
  • Eck - To show off e.g. Stahp dih eck; You stay eck bredda
  • Cho! - Can mean wow or congratulations.

Sayings in Belizean Kriol

  • Haad aaz pikni guh da maaket two time - Children that don't listen go to the shop twice.
  • Stick bruk eena yuh aaz?- Did a stick break in your ears?
  • Blood tika dan wata but wata tase betta - Blood is thicker than water but water tastes better.
  • Cut da lang bench shaat - Put an end to the long conversation.
  • Yuh si mi crass! - You see the trials I'm being put through (like Jesus on the cross)?
  • If ih nuh bun ih nuh done - If the food is not burnt, it's not done
  • Fisha man neva seh e fish stink - Means that a person will never talk badly about himself or his family
